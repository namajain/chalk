I went to collect a courier today. There were two people there. The girl asked for my name to retrieve the courier and the guy there, misheard Naman Jain as Ramanujan.
We both started laughing. We talked for a bit and it occurred to me that I should ask him if he saw The Man Who Knew Infinity ( A movie based on the life of Ramanujan ).
But the guy looked like a village bumpkin who wouldn't know much about the topic or Hollywood movies for that matter. Fortunately I realized my mistake ( thanks to the words of a certain awesome person ) , I was letting his appearance cloud my judgement. 
I made amends immediately, as it turned out, not only had he watched the movie but he also knew much about Ramanujan. We had a nice long conversation on related topics, I was also reminded of two important qualities I want to posses, being open and being unbiased :)
